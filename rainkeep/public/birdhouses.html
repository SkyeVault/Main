Installation & Care Guidelines

Expert tips for successful birdhouse placement and maintenance in Bucks County

üî®Installation Tips

  • Timing: Install February-March before breeding season
  • Orientation: Face entrance east/southeast, away from prevailing winds
  • Predator Guards: Use pole baffles, keep 5+ feet from jumping surfaces
  • Spacing: Same species houses 100+ yards apart
  • Drainage: Ensure proper drainage holes in floor

üå°Ô∏èClimate Adaptations

  • Insulation: 3/4" thick walls for Pennsylvania winters
  • Ventilation: Adequate airflow for humid summers
  • Sun Protection: Avoid harsh afternoon sun exposure
  • Weather Resistance: Slanted roof with 2-3" overhang
  • Materials: Cedar and cypress naturally weather-resistant

üóìÔ∏èSeasonal Schedule

  • February: Install/repair birdhouses
  • March-April: First broods begin nesting
  • May-July: Peak nesting season monitoring
  • August: Second broods, continued care
  • September-October: Clean out old nests
  • November-January: Maintenance and repairs

üßπMaintenance

  • Weekly: Check for damage during nesting season
  • Monthly: Remove competing nests (sparrows, starlings)
  • Seasonally: Deep clean with mild bleach solution
  • Annually: Replace weathered components
  • Access: All models feature hinged cleaning doors

üö´Common Problems

  • House Sparrows: Remove perches, use 1.25" holes
  • Starlings: 1.25" holes too small for entry
  • Predation: Install pole guards and proper spacing
  • Overheating: Provide shade and ventilation
  • Poor Drainage: Keep entrance holes clear

üåøHabitat Enhancement

  • Native Plants: Provide natural food sources
  • Water Sources: Bird baths or shallow dishes
  • Nesting Materials: Leave natural materials nearby
  • Chemical-Free: Avoid pesticides and herbicides
  • Year-Round Care: Maintain habitat through all seasons
