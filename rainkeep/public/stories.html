Meet the Rainkeep Friends

These ideas are meant to work with the dolls and scenes in your Rainkeep fabric book. Feel free to rename anyone to match your family and your child’s imagination.

Family Characters
• Selene (Mom) – the stained glass artist at Pebble & Prism Glass Studio
• Bram (Dad) – the gentle woodworker who carves toys and furniture
• Lyric – Selene and Bram's daughter who loves exploring the village
• Tuk – the loyal dachshund who follows them everywhere

Grandparents & Their Shops
• Nana Mona – the seamstress who runs the fabric & crochet studio
• Pop Jasper – the florist who fills the village with flowers
• Granny Elen – the baker who makes sweets at the deli & café
• Grandpa Corwin – the alchemist who mixes potions and glowing tonics

Magical Friends
• Wren – the village wizard
• Starbloom – the shimmering unicorn
• Lark & Violet – two curious little fairies

Children & Companion
• Ivy – a brave little girl who loves adventures
• Myra – her imaginative friend who loves to help and plan
• Theo – a thoughtful friend who asks big questions
• Tuk – the loyal dachshund who follows them everywhere

25 Story Seeds to Explore Together

  1. The First Night in the Hobbit House

    Lyric and Ivy arrive in Rainkeep for the very first time. Together you can decide what they pack, which bed they choose, and how they feel as they fall asleep under the stained glass windows in their new bedroom.

  2. Tuk the Dachshund’s Grand Tour

    Tuk decides to explore every room in the house. Move him from the bedroom to the kitchen to the library and music room. What does Tuk sniff, discover, and accidentally knock over along the way?

  3. Morning at Pebble & Prism Glass Studio

    Selene is working on a new stained glass window for the village library. Lyric and Myra help choose the colors. Decide which shapes go into the window and who it’s secretly dedicated to.

  4. Bram’s Secret Toy Workshop

    In the woodworking studio, Bram is carving a surprise toy. Ivy, Myra, and Theo are allowed to sand, paint, and test secret drawers. What kind of toy is it: an animal, a wagon, a puzzle, or something new?

  5. Nana Mona’s Patchwork Cloak

    Nana Mona is sewing a magical cloak using tiny scraps from all over the village. Each patch can hold a memory or power: courage, kindness, giggles, or bravery on road trips.

  6. Pop Jasper’s Flower Messages

    Pop Jasper teaches Ivy and Myra how each flower can send a message. Choose which flowers to send to the wizard, the alchemist, or the grandparents and what those flowers mean.

  7. Granny Elen’s Day at the Deli & Sweet Shop

    Granny Elen needs helpers. Lyric, Myra, Theo, and Tuk take orders: soup, sandwiches, and special pastries. Decide what the “Rainkeep Special” is today and who gets the last cookie.

  8. Potions with Grandpa Corwin

    Grandpa Corwin mixes safe, silly potions: giggle drops, courage sprinkles, and sleep-tight tea. Choose three and decide what gentle magic each one does.

  9. Library Card Day

    Ivy and Myra visit the village library for their very first library cards. Decide which books they check out: a dragon story, a music book, a map book, or a recipe book for Granny Elen.

  10. A Concert in the Music Room

    Ivy plays one instrument, Myra plays another, and Tuk joins in with jingling tags. Choose which instruments they try and what kind of concert they give for the grandparents.

  11. Wren the Wizard’s Lost Spell

    Wren misplaces an important spell page. Ivy, Myra, Theo, and the fairies search the library and village shops to find it. Decide who spots the missing page first.

  12. Starbloom’s Shimmering Trail

    Starbloom trots through Rainkeep at night, leaving a faint sparkling trail. Ivy and Myra follow it from the bedroom out into the village and to a special surprise meeting place.

  13. Market Day Mix-Up

    It’s market day. Flowers, bread, fabrics, and potions all get mixed up. Decide who accidentally takes the wrong basket and how Ivy and Myra help sort everything out.

  14. Tea Party for All the Grandparents

    Ivy and Myra plan a tea party. Decide which treats Granny Elen bakes, which flowers Pop Jasper brings, what Nana Mona sews for the table, and which potion Grandpa Corwin shares.

  15. Sleepover in the Glass Studio

    Lyric and Violet spread blankets in Pebble & Prism Glass Studio. Colored light paints the walls. What colors do they see, and what gentle stories does Selene tell at bedtime?

  16. Road Trip: Forest Campfire

    The friends take a road trip to the forest. Decide who gathers sticks, who starts the campfire, and which songs they sing under the stars.

  17. Road Trip: Mountain Overlook

    Ivy, Myra, and Theo drive into the mountains. From the overlook, they see the village far below. Decide what they notice from so high up and what small treasure they bring back.

  18. Road Trip: Beach Day

    At the beach, they collect shells, build a sandcastle for Tuk, and draw shapes in the sand for Starbloom. Is the water calm or wavy? Do they stay until sunset or head home for snacks?

  19. Road Trip: Desert Caravan

    The friends travel through a soft golden desert. Maybe they meet a tiny caravan, or stop at a glowing oasis. Decide what they pack and what secret Ivy keeps tucked in her pocket.

  20. The Night the Lights Went Out

    One evening, the lanterns flicker out. Ivy, Myra, Wren, and the fairies carry candles from room to room, checking on the shops. Decide how they bring the light back.

  21. A Day Off for the Grown-Ups

    The grown-ups take a day off. Ivy, Violet, and Theo decide how to “run” the village: who bakes, who sews, who waters the plants, and who stirs the pretend potions.

  22. The Library Sleep-In

    Ivy and Myra are allowed to sleep in the library. They make a bed of cushions and blankets. Which books do they read, and do the fairies sneak in to listen to the stories?

  23. Music Room Talent Show

    Everyone prepares something small to share: a song, a joke, a magic trick, a dance, or a new recipe. Decide what each character’s “talent” is and who claps the loudest.

  24. Goodnight, Rainkeep

    At the end of the day, Selene and Lyric visit each room to say goodnight: the glass studio, the shops, the kitchen, the music room, and finally the bedroom. Choose one thing each character is grateful for.

Use these prompts as a starting point and let your child lead. There is no wrong way to tell a Rainkeep story.
