DevLog 250817 — The Curious Case of the King's Oak Referrer

> Log Date: 250817

A victory in cyber-tracking. Someone clicked a ghost link and woke the logs. This is how we traced a private site leak back to an unspoken link — and proved the value of reading your own server data.

Not long ago, the cozy corner of the internet where our woodworking portfolio quietly sat started acting suspicious. A private page. No shares. No mentions. And somehow... it got visited. By exactly the person we didn’t want seeing it.

Scene 1: The Panic Before the Ping

No breadcrumbs. No social trail. We checked DNS, backlinks, bots. Nothing. It wasn’t public. So how did someone land on it like they were following a map we never drew?

Scene 2: Installing the Bug

Matomo. Self-hosted, no fluff. Installed like a scalpel, not a net. We wanted to trace the steps. If there were any. Initially, just bots. Crawlers doing crawler things. Then a blip: 

Referrer: portfolio site of a respected artist  
Path: /woodworking  
Device: Android, Chrome  
Time on page: 0 seconds  
Trust level: evaporating

The visitor came from a site we liked. One we’ve browsed before. One that... shouldn’t have had our link.

Scene 3: The Unlikely Breadcrumb

The referring site — let’s call it King’s Oak — is tasteful, minimal. But Matomo doesn’t hallucinate. The traffic came from there. We searched the DOM. Nothing. No links in the HTML. No outbound references. Static scan said clean.

So we started thinking like code.

JavaScript. Modal galleries. Hover effects. Invisible spans. Dynamic anything. Some widget was quietly linking us behind the curtain.

Scene 4: Digital Forensics

The steps became clear.

  • Someone visits the King’s Oak site.
  • A hidden or dynamic element points to our woodworking page.
  • They click. Or tap.
  • Matomo logs the hit. We get a referrer.
  • They copy the URL. They send it in an email.
  • Drama arrives in our inbox.

And that was the smoking gun. One invisible link. One chain reaction. One misrouted piece of family theater.

Scene 5: Lessons from the Log

  • Matomo is surgical. Set it up early, not after the fire starts.
  • Referrer headers are better than gossip.
  • JavaScript links live outside grep’s jurisdiction.
  • Just because you didn’t publicize it doesn’t mean the internet didn’t.
  • Read your logs. Then read them again. Then believe them.

Closing Remarks

We solved it quietly. No accusations. No confrontations. But the data doesn’t lie. The logs saw it all.

So here it is, documented in devlog form. A little too cryptic for outsiders. A little too obvious for insiders.

Referrer tracked. Mystery solved. Digital detective agency: active and always watching.
